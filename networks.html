Network Security

As a student in the MSc Computer Science program, I have undertaken a comprehensive module of Network Security aimed at mastering the intricacies of securing networks. This e-portfolio documents my journey through the module, capturing my learnings and experiences across various units.

Throughout the Network Security module, I explored multiple facets of securing network environments, gaining both theoretical knowledge and hands-on experience to address current and emerging security challenges.

Main Goals for the Module:

  1. Develop the ability to pinpoint security threats and vulnerabilities within network systems.
  2. Learn to determine appropriate methodologies, tools, and techniques to manage or resolve these issues.
  3. Design and evaluate computer programs and systems aimed at managing and auditing risk and security issues.
  4. Collect and integrate information from various sources, including internet security alerts and warning sites, to systematically analyze security breaches and issues.
  5. Understand and articulate the legal, social, ethical, and professional challenges faced by information security and risk professionals.

01 UNIT: History of Network Security, Vulnerabilities, and Approaches

In the first unit, I delved into the history of network security, tracing the origins and evolution of vulnerabilities and attacks. The Cyber Kill Chain model proposed by Lockheed Martin was a focal point, providing a framework to understand and mitigate contemporary breach approaches.

This unit provided a solid foundation in how security threats have evolved over time and how historical insights inform current defense strategies.
